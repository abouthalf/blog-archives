Tuna Croquettes

Recipes
Saturday, October 20th, 2012
Tweet

“Tuna Croquettes” sounds nicer than “Tuna Cakes” doesn’t it?

Ingredients

  • 12 oz can of tuna, drained thoroughly
  • 1 beaten egg
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 cup (approximately) bread crumbs. I use half panko, and half, uh, not panko.
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1 cup cooked, white, firm potatoes, diced small.
  • 1 shallot, finely diced
  • fresh tarragon, to taste, finely chopped.
  • dried dill, to taste
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • olive oil for frying

Equipment

  • A mixing bowl
  • A spoon
  • 2 plates
  • A sheet pan or pizza pan
  • 1/3 cup measure
  • sautée pan
  • tongs

Method

Dump the tuna into the mixing bowl and stir to break up into small chunks. Add the vegetables, tarragon, dill, salt, and pepper. Mix gently. Gently fold in the mayonnaise and beaten egg.

I save my panko for coating the outside of the croquettes, where it can be seen and crunched. I use plain breadcrumbs in the croquette itself. If you only have one type of breadcrumbs, use that.

Add enough breadcrumbs to the mixture so that it appears dry, but not so dry that it doesn’t stick together. Pour remaining breadcrumbs into one plate.

Using a 1/3 cup measure, scoop up and pack down the tuna mixture to make a small disk. Plop the disk out into your hand, then roll in the breadcrumbs, coating thoroughly. Rest the cake on the clean plate while you make the rest. I usually get 6 cakes total. One tends to be sort of big.

With the cakes formed and coated, heat a sautée pan over medium heat and add a tablespoon or so of olive oil. Using tongs, gently place the cakes into the pan. Make sure there is an inch or more in between each cake. Work in batches if you need to.

Brown each cake on one side for 2 minutes, flip it over with the tongs, and brown for 2 more minutes. Remove the cakes to a sheet pan.

When all the cakes are browned and on the sheet pan, place the pan into a 400° F oven for 10 minutes, juste long enough to heat the cakes thoroughly and set the egg.

You can serve these immediately as an appetizer. Place one or two cakes on a piece of butter lettuce on a plate. If you are feeling fancy, drizzle some hollandaise sauce on top. You can also use these in a salad as big, fancy croutons. These refrigerate well and make a good lunch. Your coworkers will be impressed.

Or you can just crunch on them while standing over the sink, you animal.

The same general techique works for poached salmon and crab as well, Mr. Rockefeller.
