Memorial day in McMinnville, OR

Things
Tuesday, May 29th, 2012
Tweet

When I was very young Bugs Bunny taught me that good Americans, when they die, go to Paris. This is a perfectly good afterlife for most Americans, but I live in Portland, Oregon. When I die, if I’m a very-very-good-boy, I’ll work at Sterling Cooper Draper Pryce and commute home to Portland to go wine tasting on the weekends. I’ll probably keep the same apartment, accept with better wiring.

My wife has a friend who lives in McMinnville, Oregon – in the heart of Oregon wine country. Her friend lent us her home for the Memorial day weekend. We drove down to McMinnville, stopping at Burgerville on the way, on Saturday afternoon. We got down to the business of not doing much immediately.

We met up with friends and friends of friends at a restaurant where the friends and friends of friends were just finishing a late, lazy lunch. We left together and ambled through downtown McMinnville to Dominio Wines. Our host had a dog and funky orange glasses. We sampled their wines in the sun on their patio. Smack may have been talked. The friends of friends were on their way to some other place, so we parted ways. We found our way over to Fino in Fondo’s tasting room. They make salumi the old fashioned way from local hogs. My favorite had a hint of truffle.

My wife’s friend recommended we stop by Remy Wines – they have only recently opened a tasting room. Like a lot of Oregon wineries, the tasting room is a garage with a board placed across two wine barrels; wine served on top. The place was crowded. A bus-load of bachelorettes showed up to tipple. We talked and sipped and suddenly I found myself talking to Remy herself. Remy is a surprisingly young looking woman with a shock of wild platinum blonde hair and a down-to-earth attitude. She was fun and answered my inane questions with grace and humor. She also makes lovely wine.

We finished the day by stopping at the local market to by beef and cream so I could make a meal fit for the big red wine we brought home. I grilled a rib-eye and sliced it thin. I grilled asparagus, and made fettucini Alfredo the right way (not too heavy).

The following day, we shared breakfast and the friend left us alone with the cat and the wine and the wineries. We decided to drive out to tiny Kristn Hill winery (too small for a web site even) and found the owner and winemaker out front passing out drink tickets and branded wine glasses for a measly $5. Eric makes some wonderful sparkling wines and some very nice port. Kristin Hill is a family affair. Brothers and nephews and such were all on hand to help us out and tell us stories and show us around their home-turned-winery.

Afterwards, we looked up and drove to Amity Vineyards. Amity Vineyards is a farm building surrounded by vineyards. The interior of the building has a one corner devoted to salesmanship, with a bar and product display. The rest of the room is devoted to storing wine in between tastings. Also: They have a dog! He’s a goo-boy.

The Blue Goat restaurant is close by in Amity, Oregon. They source almost everything locally and bake excellent pizzas in the massive brick oven centered right in the dining room.

We finished they day by finishing wine by the fire pit in our friend’s back yard. All a 45 minute from home.
