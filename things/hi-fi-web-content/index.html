For Amusement Only: the life and death of the American arcade

Snow Fall – The Avalanche at Tunnel Creek

These two articles are great; good reads and interesting topics. I hope, also, they might represent a trend towards hi-fi web content.

Both articles make use of custom typefaces and big, beautiful photographs. Both have responsive designs which scale from desktop computers to mobile devices. They integrate advertising directly into the articles, instead of wrapping ads around the content. Both take advantage of whiz-bang html5 features to integrate video, slideshows, and other interactive content. There are no app-like features, fake pagination, or other silliness. Each article is just a just single column of content, stacked in order, from top to bottom, optimized for reading while being handsome to look at.

The combination of good content plus good art direction makes for an experience much like reading a high-end print magazine, except of course that it’s interactive – you can share it on Twitter or leave a comment. Both articles were shared widely across blogs and social media and received much praise. They probably earned their advertisers a lot of money. This tells me that there’s a demand for higher quality content on the web that most cookie-cutter content management systems simply aren’t delivering.

This is the kind of content that the various “magazine” apps on iOS and Android should have been delivering all along, not silly approximations of the print magazine bundled into a huge, clunky slideshow by overpriced Adobe software. Because these two feature articles are “just” web pages, we’ll be able to dig them out of the archives years from now and revisit them, even if their hosting websites are long-since gone. Will anyone be able to launch a magazine app in 20 years and read the content?
