eBooks or AppBooks

Thibaut Sailly at Bureau has reviewed the recent e-book “Our Choice” and now I’m glad I don’t have to buy it.

I am not so much a fan of the ‘multimedia book app’ thing. We have those already, they’re called web pages. I realize there are technical advantages to running native code for a device and that HTML in a browser may be a sub-optimal experience by comparison, but the utility and device independence of the web format is far more important than hardware accelerated page transitions.

I love e-books in general. I love the Kindle app on my iPad in particular, and iBooks is OK (I’d like to be able to turn off the page animation and reduce or remove some of the ‘book chrome’ around the display.) I read far more now that I have eBooks always near me on my phone or my iPad.

I might argue with Thibaut on one point though. I don’t think paper books are here to stay for much longer. All of the problems he lists for e-books are real but solvable. The ability to have a vast library in a cheap, low power device like a kindle is the printing revolution squared. The problems he mentions are silly inconveniences in comparison.

Dishonest marketing is dishonest

Dishonest marketing is dishonest, it does not matter how great the product is.

There has been a minor kerfuffle between Merlin Mann (of 43 Folders fame) and @iA (the makers of Writer for iPad) over “distraction free writing environments”.

Merlin makes the point that making a great writing tool like Writer, is great, but implying that your software fixes a problem between the keyboard and the chair is maybe not really honest:
