Getting Around

Central Portland is easy to navigate. Burnside St. divides the city into North and South, the Willamette river divides the city into East and West. So, if you're on Burnside in the afternoon with the sun in your face, you're heading west.

The area of the city where we live, and where the ceremony and reception are taking place are laid out in a pretty reliable grid, so chances are: if you miss a street just turn at the next block and back-track to get to where you were going.

Public Transportation

Portland public transportation is very good. Depending upon which part of town you live in, you can avoid driving for days at a time.

The bus system is very reliable and inexpensive. There are light rail lines running on major axes through town, including from the airport right into downtown (passing the Lloyd Center area).

In NW Portland (near Michael’s) or a number of places downtown, you can catch the Portland Streetcar to visit NW 23rd and downtown shopping.

You can plan a trip and even buy tickets in advance at http://www.tri-met.org
