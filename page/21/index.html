Sunday, December 2nd, 2012
Tweet

So hey, I’d like to go see a motion picture at my local cinema, why don’t I use that fancy Fandango app I read about on TV. I hear I can purchase tickets right from my phone without waiting in line. Sounds great.

Aha there’s that new Jimmy Bond movie I heard about. I should tap on that so I can purchase a ticket.

And after I tap the movie I want?

B-bu-but I just tapped on this movie. Clearly I’m interested in this…why show me an ad Fandango? Why>

Yes. Fandango shows me an ad for the very same movie I selected. Useful! I guess it’s slightly less annoying than when you tap on a movie and get an ad for something completely unrelated. The first time I tapped on “Skyfall” I got an ad for “Twighlight”.

Featuring a new movie in the app, for reasons of paid placement or simply because it’s new or popular, makes sense. But why on earth would you interrupt me with an ad when I’m on the way to make a purchase?

“Wait! Don’t buy now, you could see this crap instead!”
