Facebook in the news

Development
Tuesday, September 27th, 2011
Tweet

Facebook is starting a Political Action Committee. (Facebook, and most major tech companies employ lobbyists – Facebook has been ramping up their presence in DC)

Facebook is scaring Dave Winer – Dave Winer writes about ‘frictionless  sharing’ – a feature of where sites who partner with Facebook can automatically post to your news feed (and therefore to your friends and creepy  subscribers)

Nik Cubrilovic does a little testing and notes that  you never really log out of Facebook – meaning that all the “like” buttons you see on other sites know who you are and what you’re looking at even if you’re not logged in. A person commenting on that article claims to be Facebook engineer; he claims that Facebook never uses this data for tracking, but this Wall Street Journal article contradicts that stating the data is used and then totally deleted. You can trust us.

After a couple of days, and a lot of noisy web traffic, Facebook works with Nik Cubrliovic to explain itself and it’s cookies and fixes a bug (which left your user id in an undeleted cookie)

If you can’t quit Facebook, because otherwise there’s no way to get Gramma’s cookie recipes, you should consider using a browser extension like Incognito which blocks all this cookie tracking nonsense.

Shawn Blanc saw the same study I did and found this great looking Mac app for scheduling those breaks.

Cornell University Ergonomics study recommends that standing at your desk for computer work isn’t all that it’s cracked up to be.

It’s a short article – here’s the nut: Stand up after every 20 minutes and move for 2 minutes. Sitting all day is obviously bad for you, but standing all day isn’t much better.

Purging and consolidating

Things
Sunday, September 4th, 2011
Tweet

I finished a little project this weekend. I had a rickety file drawer next to my desk. This file drawer came to live with me when I moved to Portland. It came disassembled in a flat box. Some assembly was required. It looked handsome enough on the web site. “Sturdy steel construction”, it said. It took way too long to put together. Lots of fiddly bits.

Over time and one apartment transition its parts began to buckle under the weight of the files. Something loosened. I never did find that one screw. A solid shove would send it into wobbly convulsions.

I purchased some of that industrial wire shelving. You know the stuff, rock solid and heavy. I also took the opportunity to purge a bunch of old junk and consolidate my files.

Pro tip: Mark shelf locations with tape before you assemble

USB to ADB to WTF

Office for Mac: the bright orange years

I found all sorts of old crap: Disks from long forgotten software, a copy of Mac OS 9, grade sheets from my teaching days, a USB to ADB converter (maybe I can sell that to a keyboard fetishist on eBay), and cancelled checks from accounts that have been closed for years. All this junk filled a cardboard banker’s file box to the top.

Crap, Crap, Crap, Crap

The number of files a person has grows to fill the number of file cabinets a person has. So now with less file capacity, surely I will stay more organized in the future. Surely.

The results are wheeled and lovely.

Now that I have a stack of steel (formerly a file cabinet), a pile of CDs, and 2 cubic feet of paper to shred, I need to do some recycling:

  1. Far West Fibers has many locations throughout portland where you can drop off metals, plastics, and small appliances.
  2. Free Geek is an awesome organization that recycles e-waste (including CDs) for a small donation. Usable computers and printers are refurbished by volunteers and given to charitable organizations and other needy causes.
  3. AccuShred NW has a drop-off shredding services (pay by the box) and partners with several businesses to provide additional drop-off locations.

Do you dare take the bedbug challenge?

It started with a table. A ratty table whose laminate top was peeling apart. “Free – great for patio” was scrawled on a sign taped to the top of the table. Yes, place this table on your patio where the wind, rain, and sun can finish what was started.

After this table, there was another table. Then the mattresses came. And then some more mattresses. And the then the couches.

That first table broke the seal. Now when lazy college brats move out of the apartment their parents pay for, they can all just leave their crap on the side of the road without fear of reprisal.

Thanks table guy!
